domain - A concept (like email address or social security number) whose physical examples/instances are typically captured in columns of data within databases. Some domains are more generic while others can be specific to industries or lines of business.


domain curation - The process of defining, continuously updating and maintaining the definition of a domain to keep it relevant to the current data landscape. Generally, this definition can be expressed in terms of data related aspects like synonyms, patterns, regular expressions and conceptual models.


Why is domain curation a challenge?


The first step we took towards systematically solving the domain curation challenge was to establish the various actors involved in and around domain curation, then focus on people who are users of current systems which try to enable domain curation. Through a structured user goal identification activity we modelled system users (roles not personas),  their key goals and tasks we believe they should undertake in an ideal system.

This is the first part of a series of exercises focussed on making data better. For organisations, especially large ones, with complex data landscapes. With databases in the tens of thousands and applications without accurate data inventory. Subsequent parts of the exercise will look at areas like data lineage, ontology then quality and access control. We are attempting to take a fresh look at tools for making data better - from a dimension of meaning rather than just the physical dimension of schemas, tables and columns.
